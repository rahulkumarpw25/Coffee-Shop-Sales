# Coffee-Shop-Sales
Key Sales Insights
Total Sales (May):  
Strong revenue generation in May, with clear visibility into MoM growth vs April.

May sales increased compared to April, showing positive momentum.

Rounded KPI outputs (e.g., “12.3K”) make trends easy to communicate.

Order Volume:

May had a higher number of transactions than April, confirming demand growth.

The MoM order growth percentage highlights operational scaling needs.

Quantity Sold:

Total units sold in May rose compared to April.

Indicates both higher customer traffic and larger basket sizes.

📈 Trend Analysis
Daily Sales vs Average:

Certain days in May performed above average (likely weekends or promotions).

Other days dipped below average, suggesting weekday slowdowns.

Sales by Weekday/Weekend:

Weekends outperform weekdays in sales volume.

This insight supports targeted weekend promotions and staffing adjustments.

Hourly Sales:

Peak hours around morning (8–10 AM) and late afternoon (4–6 PM).

Aligns with coffee consumption patterns — morning rush and evening breaks.

Cumulative Trend:

Sales show a steady upward trajectory across May, indicating consistent demand.

🏪 Segmentation Insights
By Store Location:

Certain branches dominate revenue, while others lag.

Opportunity: replicate best practices from top-performing stores.

By Product Category:

Beverages (coffee, specialty drinks) drive the majority of sales.

Food items contribute but are secondary — potential upsell opportunity.

Top 10 Products:

A small set of products (likely signature coffees) generate disproportionate revenue.

Focus marketing and inventory on these high performers.

💡 Strategic Insights
Operational:

Staff scheduling should prioritize weekends and peak hours.

Inventory planning must align with top-selling products and categories.

Marketing:

Promotions can target slow weekdays to balance demand.

Loyalty programs around top products can maximize repeat purchases.

Growth:

Positive MoM growth suggests scalability.

Expanding successful product lines or replicating high-performing store strategies can accelerate revenue.
